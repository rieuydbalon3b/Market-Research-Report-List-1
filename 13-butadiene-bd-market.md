<p><p>1,3-Butadiene (BD) is produced through various methods, each having distinct market dynamics. The DMF (Dimethylformamide) method utilizes a solvent-based approach, offering higher yields and purity. The ACN (Acrylonitrile) method leverages catalytic processes for efficient BD production but may involve more complex operations. The NMP (N-Methyl-2-pyrrolidone) method focuses on a solvent-centric technique, balancing efficiency and yields. Each method impacts production costs, market demand, and regulatory considerations, shaping the overall BD market landscape.</p></p>
