<p><p>The infrared (IR) cameras market is primarily segmented into cooled and uncooled types. Cooled IR cameras utilize cryogenic cooling techniques to enhance sensitivity and reduce unwanted thermal noise, making them ideal for applications requiring high precision, such as military and scientific imaging. Uncooled IR cameras, on the other hand, operate at ambient temperatures and are generally more compact and cost-effective, making them suitable for commercial uses, such as surveillance and automotive thermal imaging. Both types serve distinct applications within the IR technology landscape.</p></p>
